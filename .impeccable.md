## Design Context

### Users
The product is for a broader audience than just a single power user. It should work for active traders and market-curious users who want fast access to dealer-gamma structure, volatility context, and actionable levels without needing to decode raw options data manually.

### Brand Personality
Premium, analytical, editorial. The interface should feel confident, legible, and high-signal rather than playful or experimental. It should help users feel oriented and informed, with enough polish to feel like a serious market tool.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction should stay premium analytics/editorial rather than terminal cosplay or loud fintech chrome. Information density is welcome, but hierarchy should remain clean and readable. The product should not look gimmicky, gamer-like, or overloaded with decorative effects. Both light and dark mode should feel intentional and refined.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with signal: the most actionable market levels and regime shifts should be visible first.
2. Explain complexity gracefully: advanced concepts should feel accessible to a broader audience through grouping, labels, and concise explanatory copy.
3. Use restrained motion: transitions should smooth state changes and reinforce hierarchy, never distract from the data.
4. Keep the product feeling premium: typography, spacing, and color should communicate serious market intelligence rather than generic dashboard boilerplate.
5. Preserve continuity across pages: navigation, state changes, and shared components should feel connected and calm.
