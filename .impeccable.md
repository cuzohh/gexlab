## Design Context

### Users
Retail traders use GEXLAB to read dealer positioning quickly during live market sessions and after the close. They need fast interpretation, reliable local-first workflows, and clear visual hierarchy while scanning multiple market structure views.

### Brand Personality
Clean, minimal, aesthetic, inclusive.

The interface should feel calm, modern, and trustworthy rather than loud, gimmicky, or overly "trader bro." Motion should feel smooth and intentional. Information density is important, but never at the cost of legibility.

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined modern trading interface with elegant dark surfaces, softened contrast, and restrained accent color. The product should feel current and polished, not old, dated, or aggressively neon. It should avoid generic dashboard tropes and preserve a crisp, premium feel across desktop and web contexts.

Anti-directions:
- No dated UI chrome or legacy enterprise styling
- No harsh neon gamer aesthetics
- No cluttered, overly busy data presentation

### Design Principles
1. Lead with clarity: primary numbers, states, and actions must be readable at a glance.
2. Use restraint: accents should guide attention, not dominate the interface.
3. Preserve consistency: charts, controls, spacing, and typography should all feel like one system.
4. Keep motion purposeful: animations should support comprehension and responsiveness.
5. Stay inclusive: contrast, focus states, sizing, and interaction affordances should remain accessible and easy to use.
